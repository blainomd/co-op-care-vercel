## PART 1: THE FOUNDING STORY

### Who We Are

co-op.care is a healthcare technology platform that powers worker-owned home care cooperatives. Headquartered in Boulder, Colorado. Founded by Blaine Warkentine. The first home care cooperative in America with enterprise-grade clinical AI, FHIR R4 interoperability, and federal program integration.

The critical distinction: co-op.care is NOT a cooperative. It is the technology vendor — the operating system — that makes cooperatives into healthcare delivery organizations. The company is structured as an LLC (the "Hard Shell") that licenses technology to locally owned worker cooperatives (the "Soft Center").

The founding insight: the traditional home care industry is structurally extractive. Standard agencies capture 40–60% of family payments as platform fees to satisfy institutional investor returns. A family pays $30/hour for care; the direct care worker receives $13–17/hour. This extraction is the direct cause of 77–79% annual turnover, which destroys care continuity and prevents any meaningful clinical outcome tracking.

co-op.care replaces the extraction layer with cooperative ownership and AI. The result: 88% of every dollar goes directly to worker-owners. AI handles the coordination overhead. Worker ownership eliminates turnover. Better continuity produces better outcomes. Better outcomes unlock institutional contracts. The virtuous cycle starts with paying people what they're worth and giving them ownership.

## PART 3: THE COOPERATIVE ECONOMICS

### How Money Flows — The Cooperative Model

```
Family pays:         $30/hour
Cooperative retains: $4/hour  (RN supervision, insurance, AI, compliance)
Caregiver earns:     $26/hour (W-2 + benefits + equity)
```

### Worker-Owner Compensation

- W-2 wages: $25–28/hour (exceeding the $17/hr Colorado HCBS base wage)
- Equity vesting: Up to $52,000 over 5 years via Internal Capital Account system
- Target turnover: <15% (vs. industry 77%)
- Revenue allocation: 88% of net proceeds to the workforce
- Benefits: Full W-2 employment eliminates AB5 regulatory risk

### Platform Margin — The 50% Recalibration

Initial model assumed 12% overhead — a severe vulnerability. Recalibrated to 50% to fund: RN clinical supervision, continuous CBI background checks, W-2 payroll compliance, liability/malpractice insurance, workers' compensation, HIPAA infrastructure, human-in-the-loop AI auditing, democratic governance friction.

This 50% margin still represents a superior value proposition vs. traditional agencies extracting 40–60% while paying poverty wages.

### The Dual Economy — Time Banking

Modeled after Japan's Fureai Kippu system:
- 1 hour of service = 1 Time Credit (strict parity)
- Non-clinical support: companionship, groceries, light maintenance, transportation
- CBI background check required before first exchange
- Offloads ~30% of low-acuity professional care demand to zero-cost reciprocity network
- Saves families ~$7,488 annually
- Primary delivery mechanism for MAHA ELEVATE lifestyle interventions

## PART 4: THE TAX ADVANTAGE ARCHITECTURE

### The IRS Publication 502 "Medical Necessity" Strategy

Under IRS rules, standard "custodial" home care is NOT a qualified medical expense. co-op.care's Clinical Director (MD/DO) + CareOS Omaha System documentation legally converts care from "custodial" to "clinical maintenance."

1. Clinical Director issues Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) during onboarding
2. This legally converts monthly care fees into qualified medical expenses
3. Traditional agencies without clinical governance cannot do this
4. Families pay with pre-tax HSA/FSA dollars — a 36% effective discount

### 2026 Pre-Tax Buckets

| Account | 2026 Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Family HSA | $8,750 (+ $1,000 catch-up if 55+) | HDHP required |
| Dependent Care FSA | $5,000 | Parent must live with employee 8+ hrs/day |
| Health Care FSA | ~$3,300 | Unlocked via Letter of Medical Necessity |

Result: $3,500/month care bill effectively costs ~$2,240/month in take-home pay.
