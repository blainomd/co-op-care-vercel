# co-op.care — Competitive Positioning & Market Landscape

---

## THE PROBLEM co-op.care SOLVES

Home care in America is broken across three axes simultaneously:

**For families:** Care is fragmented. The Alpha Daughter must stitch together companion care agencies, home health agencies, skilled nursing, hospice — each a separate relationship, separate intake, separate system. When her parent's needs change, the relationship ruptures and she starts over. She is exhausted, terrified of nursing homes, and getting no sleep.

**For caregivers:** Home care workers are underpaid, under-supported, and disposable. 1099 classification strips them of benefits and protections. Annual turnover exceeds 65–80% at traditional agencies. They have no ownership, no career ladder, no voice.

**For the system:** Fragmentation drives avoidable hospitalizations, duplicated assessments, lost clinical context, and waste. Nobody owns the longitudinal relationship. Nobody is incentivized to keep people home.

---

## HOW co-op.care IS DIFFERENT

**vs. Traditional Home Care Agencies (Home Instead, Visiting Angels, BrightSpring, etc.)**
- They serve a narrow acuity band and refer out when needs escalate = relationship rupture
- 1099 or low-wage W2 workers with extreme turnover
- No clinical architecture or longitudinal tracking
- No community wellness navigation
- Extractive ownership (private equity in many cases)
- co-op.care: full acuity spectrum, W2 cooperative ownership, Omaha System clinical framework, community navigation, no relationship rupture

**vs. Home Health Agencies (Amedisys, LHC Group, Enhabit)**
- Episodic, skilled-visit model (CMS-driven 60-day episodes)
- Enter late in the acuity curve, exit when episode ends
- No companion care, no wellness, no family training
- co-op.care: starts at wellness, stays through end-of-life, trains families, navigates community

**vs. Care Management Platforms (Honor, CareAcademy, Careswitch)**
- Technology layers on top of the same broken agency model
- Don't change the ownership structure or continuity problem
- co-op.care: the cooperative IS the model innovation, not just a tech overlay

**vs. PACE Programs**
- PACE is excellent but limited: serves only dual-eligible 55+, requires adult day center attendance, limited geographic availability
- co-op.care partners WITH PACE (sub-capitation) rather than competing
- co-op.care serves broader population (not just dual-eligible) and doesn't require day center model

**vs. Long-Term Care Insurance (LTCI)**
- LTCI is episodic, exclusionary (underwriting), disability-triggered, expensive, and disconnected from actual care delivery
- co-op.care's "Age at Home Care Insurance" framing: continuous relationship, no underwriting, starts at wellness, membership model

**vs. Concierge / High-End Care Management (Alvita, Naborforce)**
- Serve affluent families only
- No cooperative ownership
- No systemic solution for the workforce
- co-op.care: designed for middle-market families through employer benefits distribution

## STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGES

1. **Continuity moat** — No competitor serves the full acuity spectrum with the same workers in the same organization. This is architectural, not incremental.

2. **W2 cooperative ownership** — Caregivers who own the cooperative stay longer, care more, and cost less to replace. This creates a compounding quality advantage over time.

3. **Federation scalability** — Technologies LLC holds IP and payer contracts; city cooperatives handle delivery. This lets co-op.care scale geographically without losing local ownership.

4. **Clinical architecture** — Omaha System as native framework (not bolted on) enables standardized quality measurement across the federation and meaningful outcomes data for payer negotiations.

5. **Community wellness navigation with HSA/FSA eligibility** — No other home care model actively refers members to community resources AND makes them financially accessible through Medical Director LMNs.

6. **Family caregiver training** — Reduces paid care hours (cost advantage) while respecting families' desire to participate (satisfaction advantage).

7. **PACE sub-capitation entry** — Accesses the highest-value payer (CMS capitation) without the cost and regulatory burden of becoming a PACE organization.

## MARKET SIZE CONTEXT

- 10,000 Americans turn 65 every day
- ~53 million Americans are informal caregivers (many are Alpha Daughters)
- Home care market exceeds $100B annually
- Average home care costs $4,000–$8,000/month (varies by market and acuity)
- Caregiver turnover industry-wide: 65–80% annually
- PACE enrollment growing but still serves <60,000 nationally

## POSITIONING SUMMARY

co-op.care is not a better home care agency. It is a different model entirely: worker-owned, full-spectrum, community-navigating, family-training, clinically-architected cooperative care. The Alpha Daughter gets one relationship that lasts. Caregivers get ownership and dignity. The system gets better outcomes at lower cost. The cooperative structure is not a feature — it is the foundation that makes everything else possible.
