## 2. WHAT co-op.care IS

co-op.care is a federated, worker-owned home care cooperative launching in Boulder County, Colorado. It enables aging adults to remain safely at home across the full acuity spectrum — from wellness and companionship through skilled nursing and end-of-life care — without relationship rupture or outside agency referral.

The venture name is always **co-op.care** — lowercase, hyphenated. Never "Family Care Cooperative," "FCC," or "Family Care Co-op."

---

## 3. PRIMARY CUSTOMER: THE ALPHA DAUGHTER

The primary customer is the "Alpha Daughter" or "Conductor" — typically a woman aged 45–60 who is managing care for an aging parent. She is the decision-maker, the coordinator, and the one who feels the pain of fragmented care systems. The entire platform, brand, marketing funnel, and UX are built around her needs — not the aging adult as end user.

The conversion funnel targets her specifically: Care Independence Index (CII) assessment → density mapping → founding member deposit flow → onboarding.

---

## 4. THE CONTINUITY MOAT

The core competitive advantage: W2 home care workers handle escalation internally within the cooperative. When a member's acuity increases (e.g., from companion care to skilled nursing to palliative), the same cooperative serves them. There is no relationship rupture, no outside agency referral, no starting over. This continuity across the full acuity spectrum is what no traditional home care agency or registry can replicate.

---

## 5. THREE CORE SERVICE OFFERINGS

**A. Home Care Delivery**
Full-spectrum care delivered by W2 cooperative members: companion care, personal care (ADLs), skilled nursing, dementia care, palliative/end-of-life. All workers are W2 employees of the local cooperative, not 1099 contractors.

**B. Family Caregiver Training**
co-op.care trains family members on care activities they choose to perform themselves — transfers, bathing, medication management, dementia techniques, fall prevention. This is a core service offering, not an add-on or afterthought. It respects the family's desire to participate in care while ensuring safety.

**C. Community Navigation**
co-op.care aggressively refers members to community wellness models — yoga studios, fitness programs, nutrition counseling, senior centers, mental health resources — and uses the Medical Director's Letter of Medical Necessity to make these HSA/FSA eligible. This positions co-op.care as a care navigator for the entire local wellness ecosystem, not just a provider of direct care hours.

## 6. REVENUE ARCHITECTURE

**PACE Sub-capitation (Highest Margin)**
TRU PACE in Lafayette, CO is the identified PACE partner. PACE (Program of All-inclusive Care for the Elderly) provides capitated payments for dual-eligible seniors. co-op.care would sub-contract care delivery under TRU PACE's capitation. The November 2026 TRU PACE pitch is a Tier 1 milestone.

**LEAD/CARA 2027**
CMS institutional revenue expected from these newer payment models targeting home and community-based services.

**Employer Partnerships**
Primary growth channel for membership acquisition. Employers offer co-op.care membership as a benefit to employees managing aging parent care (the Alpha Daughter persona). This is positioned as "Age at Home Care Insurance" — an alternative framing to traditional Long-Term Care Insurance (LTCI).

**Founding Deposits**
$100 refundable deposit per family. 200 founding families needed to launch. This validates demand and creates community buy-in before operations begin.

## 7. FEDERATION STRUCTURE

**co-op.care Limited Cooperative Association (LCA)** — Filed March 10, 2026 in Colorado. LCA (NOT LLC) enables patron/investor member structure + Subchapter T cooperative taxation. Owns all intellectual property, technology platform (CareOS), and payer contracts. This is the scalable entity.

**City-Level Cooperatives** — Handle care delivery and W2 employment of caregivers. Each city/region has its own worker-owned cooperative. Boulder County is the first.

**Federation Layer** — Governs quality standards, brand integrity, clinical protocols, and training requirements across all city cooperatives.

## 10. PHILOSOPHICAL FRAMEWORK

Blaine operates from a deep, articulated commitment to community-based, non-extractive economic models. The cooperative structure is not a tactical choice — it is the mission. Key tenets:

- A neurochemical framework for the "well-lived life" emphasizing meaningful work, sufficiency, and community connection over wealth maximization
- Explicit rejection of extraction capitalism in favor of community ownership
- Sufficiency-based economics: enough is the goal, not maximum
- Workers owning the means of care delivery is both ethical and operationally superior
- The cooperative model produces better care outcomes because aligned incentives reduce turnover and build trust

This philosophy should inform all positioning, messaging, and strategic recommendations. Do not suggest pivoting to a traditional agency model, VC-scale growth-at-all-costs, or any structure that concentrates ownership away from workers and families.
