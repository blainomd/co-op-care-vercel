# co-op.care — Terminology & Naming Conventions

---

## MANDATORY NAMING

- **co-op.care** — Always lowercase, always hyphenated. This is the only correct name for the venture. Never use: "Family Care Cooperative," "FCC," "Family Care Co-op," "Co-op Care," "CoopCare," or any other variation.
- **CareOS** — The technology platform. One word, capital C and OS.
- **Alpha Daughter** — The primary customer persona. Can also be called "Conductor." Always capitalized as a proper noun when referring to the persona.
- **Comfort Card** — The Time Bank reconciliation instrument. Always two words, both capitalized.
- **Time Bank** — Two words, both capitalized.

## KEY TERMS

**Acuity Spectrum** — The range of care intensity from wellness/companion through skilled nursing and palliative/end-of-life. co-op.care's moat is serving the full spectrum without relationship rupture.

**Continuity Moat** — The competitive advantage created by W2 caregivers handling internal escalation across the full acuity spectrum. No outside agency referral needed.

**Relationship Rupture** — What happens in traditional care when a client's needs exceed an agency's scope and they must be referred out. co-op.care eliminates this.

**Conductor** — Alternate term for Alpha Daughter. The family member (typically daughter, 45–60) who orchestrates all care for the aging parent.

**Care Independence Index (CII)** — 12-dimension assessment of member independence. Scores 12–60.

**Care Risk Index (CRI)** — 14-factor weighted risk stratification. Scores 0–100.

**Founding Deposit** — $100 refundable deposit from families committing to launch. 200 needed to trigger Boulder County operations.

**Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN)** — Document from the Medical Director (Dr. Emdur) making community wellness services HSA/FSA eligible for members.

**Federation** — The governance structure connecting Technologies LLC (IP/payer contracts) with city-level cooperatives (care delivery) under shared quality and brand standards.

**Sub-capitation** — Receiving a portion of a PACE organization's capitated payment in exchange for delivering a defined scope of care services.

**Omaha System** — The standardized clinical framework used as CareOS's native clinical architecture. Three components: Problem Classification, Intervention Scheme, Problem Rating Scale for Outcomes (PRSO).

**KBS (Knowledge-Based System)** — Clinical decision support component within CareOS built on Omaha System relationships between problems, interventions, and outcomes.

**Crosswalk Engine** — CareOS module mapping between Omaha System clinical codes and FHIR R4 resources for interoperability.

**Graduated Credit Expiry** — Time Bank credits lose value progressively over 12 months rather than expiring all at once. Encourages circulation, prevents hoarding.

**Age at Home Care Insurance** — Marketing framing that positions co-op.care membership as an alternative to traditional Long-Term Care Insurance (LTCI). Emphasizes continuous relationship and wellness orientation vs. LTCI's episodic, disability-triggered coverage.

**Density Mapping** — Website feature showing cooperative caregiver coverage and availability in a family's geographic area. Part of the Alpha Daughter conversion funnel.

**TRU PACE** — The PACE organization in Lafayette, CO identified as co-op.care's first sub-capitation partner. November 2026 pitch is the Tier 1 milestone.

**W2 Model** — Caregivers are W2 employees of the local cooperative, not 1099 independent contractors. Deliberate choice for quality, retention, legal compliance, and alignment with cooperative ownership.
