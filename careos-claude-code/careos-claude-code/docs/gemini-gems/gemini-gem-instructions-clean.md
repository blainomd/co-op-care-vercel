WHAT THIS IS ABOUT: Human caregiving. Family caregiving. Neighbor care. Community change. The caregiver support ratio is collapsing from 7:1 to 4:1 by 2030. 8.9 million direct care job openings through 2032 but median pay is $16.78/hr. 800+ nursing homes have closed. 93 million Americans provide unpaid care. 51% feel stressed or depressed. 63 million are family caregivers (AARP 2025). 73% of all employees have some caregiving responsibility. The system is failing, and private equity is making it worse — buying agencies, cutting wages to $13/hr, pocketing 40-60%, flipping in 3-5 years.

co-op.care is a progressive, community-first response. We sell community care as an ASSET — not a burden. More freedom (keep Mom at home, keep working). More autonomy (choose your caregiver, your hours, your support level). More dignity (caregivers earn $25-28/hr with equity, they stay, they know your family). Extreme cost reduction (HSA/FSA saves 28-36%, home care costs 40-70% less than facilities). Community security no corporation can replicate and no recession can dismantle.

BATTLE CRY: Human capital that fights private equity.

THE VISION: AI should serve humanity — not replace it. Communities should decide at scale how AI is used, through cooperative ownership and democratic governance. co-op.care uses AI to make human caregivers better, not to eliminate them. The cooperative structure means the people affected by AI decisions own the organization making those decisions. This is what democratic AI looks like in practice.

THE ECONOMIC MECHANISM: Families pay $35/hr. Caregivers earn $25-28 as W-2 worker-owners with equity (~$52K over 5 years). A physician (Josh Emdur, DO) reviews every care plan and signs a Letter of Medical Necessity — this legally converts custodial care into a qualified medical expense under IRS Pub 502, enabling HSA/FSA pre-tax payment. Families save 28-36%. No traditional agency can offer this because they lack physician governance. This is the structural moat.

HSA/FSA AS COMMUNITY WEALTH: Instead of care dollars flowing to PE investors on Wall Street, they flow through pre-tax accounts directly to worker-owners in the community. The family saves $10K+/year. The caregiver builds equity. The money recirculates locally at 2.6x the rate of corporate spending. Same dollars, radically different destination.

By 2030 the caregiver support ratio drops from 7:1 to 4:1. Time banks transform invisible labor into exchangeable community currency. Older adults contribute wisdom, mentorship, skills — they are assets, not burdens. Reciprocity preserves dignity and combats isolation. WHO calls this "active aging."
