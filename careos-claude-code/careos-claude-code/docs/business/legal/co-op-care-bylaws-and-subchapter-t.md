## BOARD RESOLUTION OF co-op.care

### Adoption of Cooperative Tax Status and Subchapter T Election

WHEREAS, co-op.care (the "Association") is organized as a Limited Cooperative Association under the laws of the State of Colorado to operate on a cooperative basis for the benefit of its worker-owners; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors deems it in the best interest of the Association and its members to minimize corporate-level taxation and maximize the distribution of surplus to its patrons based on their participation in the cooperative's labor;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Association shall operate on a cooperative basis as defined by Subchapter T of the Internal Revenue Code. The Association is hereby authorized and directed to file its federal income tax returns on Form 1120-C.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Association shall establish and maintain a pre-existing legal obligation to distribute its net earnings from business done with or for its patrons as patronage dividends, in accordance with the provisions set forth in the Association's Bylaws.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the officers of the Association are authorized to take all necessary actions, including the issuance of written notices of allocation and the collection of member consents, to ensure compliance with the requirements of IRC Section 1388.

## BYLAWS OF co-op.care

### A Colorado Limited Cooperative Association

#### ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE

The name of this entity is co-op.care Limited Cooperative Association. The Association is organized to provide a resilient, non-extractive home care infrastructure that enables aging adults to remain safely at home across the full acuity spectrum. Its mission is to align the economic incentives of the caregiver with the clinical outcomes of the patient through worker-ownership, thereby defeating the extractive private equity model and establishing a permanent community care utility.

#### ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

Membership is limited to "Patron Members" who are natural persons employed as W-2 caregivers by the Association. To qualify for membership, an individual must complete the Association's proprietary training program, demonstrate competency in the Omaha System and CareOS NLP pipeline, and be approved by the Board of Directors. Each Patron Member shall possess one, and only one, vote on all matters submitted to a vote of the membership, embodying the principle of "one member, one vote."

#### ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE

The affairs of the Association shall be managed by a Board of Directors. The Board shall include worker-owners and may include external directors, such as clinical physicians, to provide clinical governance and oversight. The Board is responsible for the strategic direction of the cooperative, including the approval of the annual budget, the oversight of the CareOS Clinical AI safety layers, and the issuance of Letters of Medical Necessity (LMNs) through the Medical Director.

#### ARTICLE IV: PATRONAGE DIVIDENDS AND TAXATION

The Association shall operate so that its net earnings from business done with or for its Patron Members are allocated to them on the basis of the quantity or value of business done with the Association.

**Section 1: Patronage Defined.** For the purposes of this Association, "patronage" shall be measured by the total number of hours worked by a Patron Member in the delivery of care services or the administration of the cooperative.

**Section 2: Allocation of Surplus.** At the close of each fiscal year, the Association shall determine its net earnings. After providing for reasonable reserves for operating capital and clinical infrastructure (including Aidbox FHIR licenses and CareOS development), the remaining net earnings shall be allocated to Patron Members as patronage dividends in direct proportion to their patronage during that year.

**Section 3: Form of Distribution.** Patronage dividends may be distributed in cash, property, or qualified written notices of allocation. To comply with Subchapter T, at least 20% of each Patron Member's total annual patronage dividend shall be paid in cash. This cash component is intended to provide the member with sufficient funds to cover any personal income tax liability arising from the full allocation.

**Section 4: Consent of Members.** Each person who hereafter applies for and is admitted to membership in this Association, and each member of this Association who continues as a member after the date of the adoption of this Bylaw, shall, by such act alone, consent that the amount of any distributions with respect to patronage which are made in qualified written notices of allocation (as defined in 26 U.S.C. § 1388) and which are received by the member from the Association, will be taken into account by the member at their stated dollar amounts in the manner provided in 26 U.S.C. § 1385(a) in the taxable year in which such written notices of allocation are received by the member.

#### ARTICLE V: INTERNAL CAPITAL ACCOUNTS

The Association shall maintain a system of internal capital accounts for each Patron Member. These accounts shall record the initial membership fee, any additional capital contributions, and the portion of patronage dividends retained by the cooperative as "qualified written notices of allocation." These retained funds constitute the "Human Capital Moat" of the Association, providing the necessary liquidity to maintain operations and scale the cooperative without seeking extractive external equity.

#### ARTICLE VI: DISSOLUTION AND LIQUIDATION

Upon the dissolution of the Association, its assets shall first be used to pay all debts and liabilities. Any remaining assets shall be distributed to the Patron Members in proportion to their respective capital account balances. In keeping with the cooperative mission, no part of the Association's net assets shall inure to the benefit of any external shareholder or non-patron entity.

## Implementation Summary

1. **Tax Neutrality:** Surplus is taxed only once at the member level, preventing the 21% corporate tax drag on profits intended for caregiver wages.
2. **Labor Stability:** The capital account system creates a "lock-in" effect where worker-owners build tangible wealth over time, targeting the <15% turnover rate required for clinical safety.
3. **Regulatory Compliance:** The consent language in Article IV satisfies the strict requirements of the IRS, ensuring that the 1120-C filing is defensible under audit.
4. **Strategic Ready-State:** These documents are now prepared for Jason Wiener's final review, allowing co-op.care to proceed immediately with EIN and NPI applications.
