## TIER 1: IMMEDIATE (Apply Now — Revenue by Q3 2026)

### 1. CMS ACCESS Model
**What:** Outcome-Aligned Payments for technology-supported chronic care management. 10-year program. Recurring payments tied to measurable health improvements (BP reduction, HbA1c, depression scores), not visit volume.
**Money:** Recurring OAPs per beneficiary per condition. Exact rates TBD in RFA but estimated at $100–300/beneficiary/month based on track. Co-management payments to referring PCPs ~$100/beneficiary/year.
**Deadline:** March 20, 2026 (safe target) for July 1, 2026 cohort. Applications after April 1 considered for January 1, 2027 start.
**Fit for co-op.care:** Near-perfect. Four clinical tracks: early cardiometabolic (hypertension, obesity, prediabetes), late cardiometabolic (diabetes, CKD, CVD), musculoskeletal (chronic pain), behavioral health (depression, anxiety). Your CII/CRI + CareOS + physician oversight + FHIR R4 = exactly what they want. Josh as designated Clinical Director. You can operate in multiple tracks.
**Requirements:** Medicare Part B enrollment (CMS-855B needed — 60-90 days), designated physician Clinical Director (Josh), FHIR R4 data reporting, HIPAA compliance, state licensure compliance.
**Critical note:** You need CMS-855B enrollment to participate. Submit 855B immediately. If it doesn't clear before July 1, you join the January 2027 cohort — still valuable.
**Office Hour:** March 18, 2026 at 3:00 PM ET — API implementation and data submission. Blaine + Pavel must attend.
**Action:** Complete ACCESS Interest Form NOW. Attend March 18 Office Hour. Submit application by March 20.

### 2. Paid CII/CRI Assessments (No License Required)
**What:** Clinical assessments under physician direction. $150–300 per assessment.
**Money:** $150–300 per completed CII-12 or CRI-14 assessment. If you do 10 per week at $200 average = $8,000/month.
**Timeline:** Can start immediately. Josh is confirmed. Tools are built. No agency license required for clinical assessments under physician direction.
**Fit:** This is your first revenue. Families pay for a comprehensive clinical assessment of their loved one's care needs. Josh reviews and signs off. The output is a CRI score + care plan recommendation + LMN if appropriate.
**Requirements:** Josh's active medical license, professional liability insurance (bridge coverage from Founder Shield), informed consent process, HIPAA-compliant data handling.
**Action:** Start marketing assessments this month. $200/assessment. Target: 5 per week by April = $4,000/month while waiting for everything else.

### 3. Medicare CHI/PIN Billing
**What:** Community Health Integration (G0019/G0022) and Principal Illness Navigation (G0023/G0024) billing codes. These exist specifically for community health workers providing care coordination under physician oversight.
**Money:** $85–120/patient/month. At 50 patients = $4,250–6,000/month.
**Timeline:** Available once CMS-855B clears (60-90 days from submission).
**Fit:** Perfect. CHI/PIN were designed for exactly this model — non-physician providers doing care coordination, social needs assessment, and health system navigation under physician direction.
**Requirements:** CMS-855B enrollment, physician oversight (Josh), documentation of services, eligible diagnoses.
**Action:** Submit CMS-855B enrollment this month. Begin billing once enrollment clears.

## TIER 2: NEAR-TERM (Apply Q2 2026 — Funding by Q3-Q4 2026)

### 4. MAHA ELEVATE Model (Make America Healthy Again)
**What:** ~$100 million for 3-year cooperative agreements (up to 30 proposals) promoting health prevention for Original Medicare beneficiaries using evidence-based, whole-person care approaches including functional/lifestyle medicine interventions not currently covered by Medicare.
**Money:** ~$3.3 million per award (average), 3-year cooperative agreement.
**Timeline:** NOFO (Notice of Funding Opportunity) expected early 2026. Model launches September 1, 2026. Two cohorts — 2026 and 2027.
**Fit for co-op.care:** Strong. MAHA ELEVATE funds "whole-person care approaches" for prevention of chronic disease in Medicare populations. Your CII/CRI assessments, caregiver-supported medication adherence, fall prevention, nutrition support, and social isolation reduction (Time Bank) all qualify. The cooperative model is novel and aligns with MAHA's community-empowerment framing.
**Requirements:** Must demonstrate experience delivering interventions, data collection capability, peer-reviewed literature supporting approach, beneficiary safeguards.
**Key framing:** co-op.care addresses chronic disease through community-based prevention: caregiver-supported medication adherence ($100B non-adherence problem), fall prevention (biggest injury cause for 65+), nutrition and meal support, social connection via Time Bank (combats isolation). All physician-directed, FHIR R4 documented, outcome-measurable.
**Contact:** mahaelevate@cms.hhs.gov
**Action:** Subscribe to CMS Innovation Center listserv. Watch for NOFO. Begin preparing application narrative using ACCESS clinical governance framework.

### 5. ACL (Administration for Community Living) Grants
**What:** $60 million announced September 2025 for states, territories, tribes, and local organizations supporting older adults and Americans with disabilities. Fall prevention, chronic condition management, caregiver strategies, dementia programs, nutrition, elder justice.
**Money:** Varies by program — typically $100K-$500K per award.
**Timeline:** Rolling announcements on Grants.gov and ACL.gov.
**Fit:** Strong for specific programs: fall prevention (co-op.care's CRI tracks falls), caregiver support (CII-12 burnout assessment), RAISE Family Caregivers Act implementation (exactly what co-op.care does), senior nutrition (caregiver meal support).
**Key quote from HHS Secretary Kennedy:** "We are directing resources where they matter most — prevention, independence, and dignity." That is literally co-op.care's mission.
**Action:** Monitor ACL.gov and Grants.gov weekly for new funding announcements. Apply to each relevant NOFO as it appears.

### 6. Rural Health Transformation Program (RHTP)
**What:** $50 billion over 5 years (announced December 29, 2025) for "improving healthcare access, quality, and outcomes by transforming the healthcare delivery ecosystem." One of the largest healthcare funding packages under this administration.
**Money:** Massive — $50B total. Individual awards vary. CHART Model component offers cooperative agreements up to $5M per lead organization.
**Timeline:** Awards announced January 2026 in all 50 states. Ongoing applications.
**Fit:** co-op.care targets Boulder but expansion to rural Colorado communities is natural. Rural areas have the worst caregiver shortages and the highest need. Your "national brain, local hands" federation model is designed for this — technology from Boulder, care delivery from rural community-owned co-ops.
**Note:** CMS announced awards in all 50 states including tribal set-asides. Colorado is included.
**Action:** Research RHTP Colorado awards. Identify potential rural expansion partners. May be a 2027 application but build the relationship now.

### 8. Colorado SB24-205 Cooperative Conversion Tax Credit
**What:** 75% state tax credit for converting existing businesses to cooperative ownership.
**Money:** Tax credit (not direct funding) — but hugely valuable for the Legacy Exit pipeline. Existing home care agency owners who convert to worker cooperatives under the co-op.care federation get 75% of conversion costs as a tax credit.
**Timeline:** Available now under existing law.
**Fit:** This is the national expansion accelerator. Every Legacy Exit participant who converts their agency to a co-op under co-op.care's federation model benefits from this credit. co-op.care provides the technology, governance, and playbooks. The tax credit makes the financial case for conversion.
**Action:** Reference in all Legacy Exit conversations. Include in BVSD employer benefit proposal (cooperative benefits align with public-sector values).

### 9. SBIR/STTR (Small Business Innovation Research)

**Timeline:** Proposal after ACCESS application submitted. Pilot Q3 2026.
