## Turnover Citation — Multiple Sources

The "77% turnover" figure used in co-op.care documents draws from multiple industry sources:

| Source | Turnover Figure | Year | Notes |
|--------|----------------|------|-------|
| Home Care Pulse (now Activated Insights) | **77.1%** | 2022 | Annual Home Care Benchmarking Study |
| PHI National | **~75%** | 2024 | Varies by sub-category and methodology |
| NAHC / HomeCare Magazine | **65-82%** | Various | Range depending on agency size and region |

**Recommendation:** Use **"77%"** with citation to *Home Care Pulse / Activated Insights, 2022 Annual Benchmarking Study*. This is the most commonly cited figure and is defensible. PHI's ~75% is also valid for a more conservative claim.

## How to Use These Citations

### In OEDIT Narrative
```
According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, home health aides earned a national
median wage of $16.78/hour in May 2024 — yet at PE-owned agencies, actual
compensation ranges from $12.59 to $14.37 (BLS 10th-25th percentile). PHI National
reports that 36% of direct care workers live at or near poverty, and 49% rely on
public assistance programs. Annual industry turnover exceeds 77% (Home Care Pulse,
2022). co-op.care's $25-28/hr W-2 model with equity ownership targets <15% turnover.
```
