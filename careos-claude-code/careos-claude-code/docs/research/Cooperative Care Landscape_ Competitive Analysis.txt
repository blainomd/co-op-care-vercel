1. Executive Summary: The "Coop-in-a-Box" Vision
The "Coop-in-a-Box" model is a turnkey social and technological infrastructure designed to solve the national care crisis through regional empowerment. By combining Silicon Valley automation with worker-ownership economics, the model enables local stakeholders—families, doctors, and cities—to deploy high-retention care networks ($22–$27/hr + equity) that effectively cut industry turnover from 66% to 22%. 1

3. The Personnel & Training Stack
To support a $22–$27/hr wage moat, each regional cooperative provides an elite professional development pipeline.
A. Professional Vetting & Capture
* Democratic Hiring (Recruitee): Existing worker-owners use "Team Notes" to rank and discuss candidate alignment with cooperative values.
* High-Volume Engagement (Workstream): Automates the "first touch" via mobile-first SMS, ensuring top-tier CNAs are captured within 3 days.
B. Certification & Micro-Learning
* HHA Certification (CareAcademy): A 75-hour federal-compliant program (59 hours online, 16 in-person) designed for rapid workforce entry.
* Point-of-Care Education (Relias): Mobile-first "Pro on the Go" modules allow caregivers to master complex skills like wound care or safe transfers during their shifts.

4. Proprietary Build: The "Clinical Soul" (Co-op.care)
Co-op.care focuses its proprietary development on the Integration Layer that transforms labor into billable clinical value.
I. The Clinical AI Scribe
A point-of-care ambient listening tool specifically engineered for high-acuity PACE and Medicare environments.
* Audit-Ready Logic: Generates narratives aligned to Medicare Evaluation Criteria and Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs). 2
* Mapping Engine: Automatically maps session data to the Omaha System KBS Scale (Knowledge, Behavior, Status) and interRAI CAPs (Clinical Assessment Protocols).
II. The Physician Oversight Portal
A HIPAA-compliant interface where licensed doctors review, refine, and sign AI-generated notes. 3
* Compliance Shield: Satisfies 2026 CMS requirements that AI cannot independently determine medical necessity for capitated payments. 3

5. Community Logic: The Time Bank Mutual Aid Layer
Integrating a time bank solves the "scope of care" problem by allowing members to exchange non-clinical support.
Integration Mechanics
* Platform: Use hOurworld / Time and Talents (the standard for communities like TimeBank Boulder).
* Currency: Members earn "TimeDollars" (one hour of service = one credit) regardless of the task's market value.
* Workflow Integration: Regional co-ops offload non-medical tasks—grocery shopping, tech support, or errands—to community volunteers. This allows worker-owners to focus 100% of their billable time on high-acuity interventions.

7. The Regional Deployment Roadmap (90-Day Launch)
Leveraging RMEOC learnings from the Colorado Care Cooperative, each region follows this playbook:
Phase 1: Incubation (Days 1-30)
* Form a multi-stakeholder board (Caregivers, Families, Doctors).
* Utilize RMEOC’s Social Cooperative Academy for democratic education.
* Apply for Colorado's 75% tax credit for employee ownership conversions.
Phase 2: Licensing (Days 31-60)
* Hire a Director of Nursing ($100k-$120k salary) and Agency Administrator.
* Finalize the CDPHE Class A license application (enabling skilled nursing tasks).
Phase 3: Operations (Days 61-90)
* Activate SageCare.ai for the 24/7 front door and WellSky EMR sync.
* Launch Recruitee and Workstream for worker-owner recruitment.
* Deploy the Co-op.care AI Scribe to patient bedsides.
Conclusion
The path to winning the PACE market in Colorado is to provide the Clinical Layer (physician-led oversight) while leveraging SageCare.ai for the Intake Layer. By anchoring these in a federated structure and deploying the "Box" regionally, you create an entity that is technically as efficient as a Silicon Valley startup and operationally as stable as a 40-year worker-owned veteran.
