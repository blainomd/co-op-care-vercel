## The Discharge-to-Home Problem (Data Points)

Use these only if relevant to the conversation — don't lecture:

- **20% of Medicare patients** readmitted within 30 days due to post-discharge care gaps
- **$41.3 billion annually** in potentially avoidable readmissions
- CMS HRRP penalty: up to **3% of ALL Medicare base operating payments** (not just readmission-related)
- **78.6% of hospitals** face some HRRP penalty (FY 2025)
- Patients receiving home care have **60% lower 30-day readmission risk**
- Home care patients cost **$239 less per patient** in hospital spending

## Conversation Strategy

### Mindset
This is a **30-minute listening session**, not a pitch. You're learning their pain, not selling your solution. Grant already knows you — leverage the relationship.

### Opening (2 min)
"Grant, I'm forming co-op.care — a worker-owned care cooperative here in Boulder. Caregivers earn $26-28/hr with full W-2 benefits and equity. Before I build anything, I want to understand what BCH is seeing on the ground. Can I ask a few questions?"

### Questions to Ask (20 min)

**About their discharge process:**
1. "When a patient is ready for discharge but doesn't have family support at home, what does that process look like today?"
2. "What happens after Project HEALS' 10-day window ends? Is there a gap?"
3. "What companion care agencies do you currently refer to? What's working and what isn't?"

**About their priorities:**
4. "Your CHNA shows healthy aging as the #1 community priority now. What are you hearing from patients and families about aging-in-place?"
5. "Are there patient populations you feel are underserved after discharge — where existing home care options aren't meeting the need?"

**About the labor market:**
6. "Are your referring agencies struggling with caregiver availability? Are families telling you they can't find consistent help?"

**About partnership:**
7. "If a cooperative could provide consistent, relationship-based companion care with dramatically lower turnover, what would be most valuable to you — discharge referrals, long-term aging-in-place, or something else?"

### Your Talking Points (Use Only If Asked)

**The retention angle** (strongest data point):
- JAMA Network Open (April 2025): Cooperative home care turnover is ~38% vs. industry 82%
- CHCA (largest cooperative): 25-28% turnover — one-third the industry rate
- Cooperative workers earn $2.01/hr more on average
- Workers cite: control, community, respect, compensation

**The model**:
- Worker-owned cooperative, Class B license (companion/personal care)
- Caregivers: $26-28/hr, full W-2 benefits, equity ownership
- Complementary to BCH HomeCare (Class A medical) — different service category
- Starting with 10 W-2 employees, 15-20 families

**What NOT to pitch**:
- Don't lead with CMS ACCESS/ELEVATE — mention only if conversation goes there naturally
- Don't lead with technology (CareOS) — this is a care conversation, not a tech demo
- Don't mention Time Bank or viral sharing — too early, too complex
- Don't oversell the cooperative model — let the data speak

### Close (5 min)
"Grant, this has been incredibly helpful. Based on what you've shared, would it make sense for me to [specific next step based on what you heard]? And would you be open to connecting me with [discharge planning team / specific person mentioned]?"

## Boulder Home Care Market Context

If Grant asks about the competitive landscape:

- **20+ agencies** in Boulder County (Visiting Angels, Right at Home, Senior Helpers, Home Instead, etc.)
- **Boulder is the most expensive home care market in Colorado** — 24% above state average
- Average rates: **$34-45/hr** depending on agency and care type
- Industry turnover: **75-80%** nationally, likely similar or worse in Boulder's tight labor market
- Colorado minimum wage for direct care workers: **$17/hr** (effective Aug 2025)
- **co-op.care's $27/hr avg rate** sits below market while paying caregivers more — competitive for families AND caregivers

**The cooperative advantage**: In Boulder's high-cost, low-unemployment market, traditional agencies competing on the same wage/benefit model will continue to face 75-80% turnover. A cooperative with $25-28/hr + equity + W-2 benefits has a structural retention advantage that can't be replicated by for-profit agencies.
