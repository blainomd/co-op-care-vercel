The Problem BCH Faces Today

Every day, Boulder Community Health discharges patients to home care agencies that:

  • Rotate caregivers constantly (79% annual turnover) — destroying therapeutic relationships built during the inpatient stay
  • Produce no structured clinical documentation — leaving BCH blind to post-discharge outcomes
  • Have no FHIR interoperability — meaning BCH's care plans disappear at the door
  • Cannot participate in CMS value-based programs — eliminating any shared savings upside for BCH
  • Have zero financial incentive to prevent readmissions — in fact, readmissions generate agency revenue
The result: BCH carries the full cost and reputational risk of readmissions while the downstream care partner carries none of it — and the patient gets the worst of both worlds.

How the Partnership Works — Operationally

Step 1: BCH Discharge Coordination

BCH discharge coordinators identify Medicare FFS patients (CKM, eCKM, MSK, or BH tracks) appropriate for home care. Referral goes to co-op.care SageCare.ai intake (24/7, auto-drafted care plan within hours).

Step 2: Caregiver Assignment & FHIR Handoff

co-op.care assigns a worker-owner caregiver with continuity commitment. BCH's discharge care plan is ingested via FHIR R4 (Aidbox backend). No data loss at transition.

Step 3: Ongoing Clinical Documentation

Caregiver ambient voice notes → AI maps to Omaha System KBS (1–5) → Physician validation (Josh Emdur, DO) → Structured FHIR R4 data. BCH receives structured outcome data back in real time — closing the loop for quality reporting.

Step 4: ACCESS Model Billing & Shared Outcomes

co-op.care bills CMS ACCESS Model OAPs ($180–$420/beneficiary/year). Outcome Attainment Rate data is shared with BCH for population health reporting. Readmission rates tracked jointly.

BCH letter of support for ACCESS application: A formal letter of support from BCH as a discharge channel partner materially strengthens co-op.care's CMS ACCESS Model application (due April 1, 2026). This is a low-lift action for BCH with high impact for both organizations.
