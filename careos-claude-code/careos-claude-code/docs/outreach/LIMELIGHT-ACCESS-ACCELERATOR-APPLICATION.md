## The Problem

The typical family caregiver is a 49-year-old woman working full-time while providing 27 hours of weekly care for an aging parent. She has no infrastructure, no physician backing, no tax advantages, and no community. When she burns out, her parent goes to a facility at $9,900/month. Everyone loses.

## The Solution

Five integrated care sources under one physician's Letter of Medical Necessity:
1. **The Conductor** — trained family caregiver with AI dashboard
2. **Time Bank** — neighbors exchanging care hours at $0
3. **W-2 Personal Care Workers** — $25-28/hr + cooperative equity
4. **W-2 Skilled Care Workers** — RN, CNA, PT, OT, SLP + equity
5. **Community Wellness Referrals** — prescribed by the physician

One physician oversees all five. Zero handoffs. The family never retells the story.

## ACCESS Model Alignment

co-op.care is purpose-built for CMS ACCESS:
- **Outcome-aligned payment** — our Omaha-coded documentation + passive PROMIS inference generates the outcomes data ACCESS evaluators need
- **Care coordination** — single physician oversight across five care sources
- **Cost reduction** — structured community care reduces avoidable readmissions and facility admissions
- **Equity** — cooperative ownership model addresses workforce turnover (77% industry → target 15%)
- **Technology** — AI clinical documentation at $0.08/visit (Whisper + Claude Omaha parser)

**Target:** CMS ACCESS Cohort 2, January 2027 (revised from April 2026 Cohort 1 to allow operational proof)
