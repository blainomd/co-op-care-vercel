# BCH Conversation Prep — Grant Besser, BCH Foundation
**Meeting Type:** 30-minute discovery conversation (NOT a pitch)
**Your Role:** Listen 80%, talk 20%
**Goal:** Understand how BCH and their patients' families currently navigate home care — and whether a reliable companion care cooperative would be a useful referral resource.

---

## PRE-MEETING RESEARCH CHECKLIST
Complete these 3-5 days before the meeting:

- [ ] **BCH patient demographics** — What % of their patients are 65+? What conditions drive most admissions?
- [ ] **Current social work / case management** — Who helps families find home care after discharge? What tools do they use?
- [ ] **Local home care landscape** — Who provides companion care in Boulder now? Visiting Angels? Home Instead? Local agencies? Are families happy with them?
- [ ] **BCH Foundation priorities** — What does Grant's Foundation focus on? Community health? Aging? Social determinants?
- [ ] **Grant Besser background** — LinkedIn. What's his track record? What kinds of community partnerships has he championed?
- [ ] **CMS/value-based participation** — Is BCH in MSSP ACO, BPCI, or any value-based programs? (Tells you if they think about post-acute outcomes)
- [ ] **Recent BCH news** — Google "Boulder Community Health" + "aging" or "home care" or "community partnerships"
- [ ] **Shared contacts** — Do you know anyone else at BCH who could warm the intro?

## YOUR OPENING SCRIPT (30 seconds — memorize this)

> "Grant, thanks for making time. I'm building a companion care cooperative here in Boulder — worker-owned, focused on giving aging adults reliable, long-term support so they can stay home. I've spent 20 years in healthcare technology, built BrainLAB's orthopedic vertical to $250M, and I've been working on the caregiving problem for over a decade. But honestly, I'm here because I want to understand: when families come to BCH needing help caring for an aging parent — whether it's after a hospital stay or just because things are getting harder at home — where do you send them? And are you happy with those options?"

**Key moves:**
- Frame as companion care (not discharge crisis)
- Credibility in one sentence (BrainLAB, decade in caregiving)
- Immediately ask THEM a question — let them talk

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (in conversation order)

### Opening — Get Them Talking (pick 2-3)
- "When families call BCH looking for help with an aging parent, what's the most common situation?"
- "Where does your team typically refer families who need ongoing home support — not necessarily skilled nursing, but companion-level care?"
- "Are you happy with the home care agencies in Boulder? What do families tell you about their experiences?"
- "Does the Foundation get involved in aging-in-place or caregiver support programs?"

### Deepening — Understand the Pain (pick 2-3)
- "When families use these agencies, do you hear about caregiver turnover? Different person every week?"
- "Are there families who need help but can't afford traditional agencies? What happens to them?"
- "For your discharge planning team — is finding reliable home care for non-clinical needs (companionship, meals, errands) a bottleneck, or does that mostly work itself out?"
- "What about your own employees? Do you have staff dealing with aging parents? Is that something the Foundation thinks about?"

### Solution-Fit — Plant the Seed (pick 1-2)
- "If there were a reliable companion care cooperative in Boulder — caregivers earning $25-28/hr as W-2 employees and cooperative owners, specifically designed for long-term relationships — would that be something you'd want to know about as a referral option?"
- "One thing we're testing is whether the cooperative model — worker ownership — actually solves the turnover problem. If we could show you that data in 6 months, would that be interesting?"

### Next Steps — Earn the Follow-Up
- "Who else at BCH should I be talking to about this? Case managers? Social workers? Your discharge planning team?"
- "Is this something you'd want to revisit once we have our first cohort of caregivers serving families?"
- "Would it help if I sent you a one-pager you could share with your team?"

## WHAT NOT TO SAY

- "I'm a doctor / physician / orthopedic surgeon" — You're an MD with deep clinical training, but NOT a licensed practicing physician. Say "healthcare technology strategist" or just focus on BrainLAB credential.
- "We solve your discharge crisis" — Too aggressive. You're building a companion care resource, not claiming to fix their clinical operations.
- "We're launching with [specific partner]" or "BVSD" — Don't claim partnerships that don't exist.
- "Blockchain" / "tokens" / "Web3" / "smart contracts" — Don't mention any of this. Ever.
- "35+ patents" — Not verifiable. Don't bring up patents at all unless asked.
- Specific revenue projections or savings numbers — You're pre-launch. Be honest about that.
- Don't talk about yourself for more than 2 minutes total. This is about THEIR experience and THEIR patients' families.

---

## WHAT TO LISTEN FOR

### Green Lights (lean in)
- "We don't have a good referral option for companion care" — BINGO. That's your opening.
- "Families complain about caregiver turnover" — Your thesis is validated.
- "We've been thinking about aging-in-place programs" — The Foundation may be a natural partner.
- "Let me connect you with our social work team" — They're opening the door.
- "How does the cooperative model work?" — They're genuinely curious. Explain briefly.
- "Our employees deal with this too" — Employer benefit angle opens up.

### Yellow Flags (note but don't react)
- "We're happy with our current home care partners" — Ask: "What works well about them?" (learn what they value)
- "The Foundation doesn't handle clinical operations" — Ask: "Who does? Can you introduce me?"
- "This would need to go through [specific person]" — Write down the name. Ask for an intro.
- "We're not looking to add partners right now" — Ask: "When would be the right time to revisit this?"

### Red Flags (don't push — just learn)
- "We don't see home care as part of our scope" — Fundamentally different vision. Note it, move on to other health systems.
- "We tried something like this and it didn't work" — Ask what happened. Learn from their failure.
