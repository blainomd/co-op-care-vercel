## Prompt 1: Homepage Redesign (paste first)

Design a mobile-first landing page for "co-op.care" — a worker-owned home care and wellness cooperative in Boulder, CO.

Brand colors: Sage green #2BA5A0 (primary), Navy #1B3A5C (headings), Warm white #FAFAF8 (background), Gold #C49B40 (accents).
Fonts: Literata (serif, headings), DM Sans (sans-serif, body).

The page should flow like this:
1. Clean nav bar with a leaf/sprout logo mark + "co-op.care" wordmark. A "Get started" pill button on the right.
2. Hero section with the headline "Your family deserves real help, not another Google search at midnight." Subtitle: "co-op.care connects families with trusted caregivers who stay. A doctor oversees everything. Yoga and wellness are part of the plan."
3. A white card in the center with three big buttons: "I'm caring for a loved one", "I need care for myself", "I want to give care" — each with a small icon.
4. A "How it works" section (4 steps with circular icons): Talk to Sage → We figure out what you need → Meet your caregivers → A doctor watches over it all.
5. Story cards that expand on tap: "She's up at 2am wandering", "He keeps forgetting his pills", "I can't keep doing this alone", "My back is killing me. I never exercise anymore." — each with colored tags like Sleep, Safety, Yoga, Burnout.
6. Dark navy stats section: 63M family caregivers, 27hrs unpaid care/week, 85% caregiver retention, 48hrs from call to care.
7. Six differentiator cards: (1) Caregivers own the company, (2) A real physician for $59/mo, (3) Clinical data from every visit, (4) Yoga and wellness are care not extras, (5) The community builds itself, (6) Voice is the interface.
8. Final CTA: "Take the 30-second check" button.
9. Simple footer with logo, "Boulder, Colorado · Pre-launch 2026".

Feeling: Warm, calm, trustworthy. Like a friend who's a nurse sitting next to you. NOT corporate healthcare, NOT startup-y. Think Headspace meets One Medical meets a cooperative grocery store.

## Prompt 2: Sage AI Chat Interface

Design a mobile chat interface for an AI care companion called "Sage." This lives below a visual care card.

Top half: A "Comfort Card" showing the user's care profile — their name, who they're caring for, a visual bloom/flower that grows as they engage more. Sage green gradients, soft rounded corners.

Bottom half: A chat interface with:
- Sage's messages in light sage green bubbles (left-aligned)
- User messages in white bubbles (right-aligned)
- "Thinking" state shows 3 animated steps: "Checking your profile..." → "Reviewing care options..." → "Preparing response..."
- Below each Sage message: 2-3 follow-up chip buttons the user can tap (e.g., "What about yoga?", "How much does it cost?", "I need respite")
- Action cards that appear inline: a card with icon + title + description + button (e.g., "Wellness Check — 30 seconds, 3 sliders — Start now")
- Text input at bottom with send button and mic icon for voice

Colors: sage #2BA5A0 for Sage's personality, navy #1B3A5C for user elements, warm white #FAFAF8 background.
The vibe is conversational and warm — like texting a knowledgeable friend, not a medical chatbot.

## Prompt 3: Yoga & Wellness Landing Section

Design a dedicated section (could be a standalone page or a homepage section) for co-op.care's yoga and wellness offering.

Headline: "Wellness that's prescribed, not optional."
Subtitle: "Yoga, movement, and mindfulness — part of your care plan, backed by a doctor, payable with pre-tax health savings."

Include:
- Two columns: "For You (the caregiver)" and "For Your Loved One" — each with 4 wellness activities (yoga, stretching, breathing, mindfulness)
- A visual showing the HSA/FSA savings: "$1,000/month care → save $300/month with HSA" — use a simple bar chart or comparison
- A card about the Respite Fund: "When your burnout score hits the red zone, our Respite Fund covers yoga classes, meditation apps, massage — anything that helps you recover."
- Testimonial-style quote: "I didn't think I'd ever have time for yoga again. Now it's part of Mom's care plan — and mine."
- CTA: "Check your burnout level" button

Colors: sage green primary, warm whites, soft imagery feel (no stock photos — use abstract shapes, leaves, gentle gradients). Think wellness app meets clinical credibility.

Include:
- 3 stat cards at top: $2,500/day blocked bed, 15.4% readmission rate, 85% caregiver retention

- 5 differentiator cards: Worker-owned caregivers, Structured health data, Physician oversight, Zero upfront cost, Wellness-integrated care

Professional but not cold. Data-driven but human. Think: health system innovation pitch, not enterprise SaaS.
