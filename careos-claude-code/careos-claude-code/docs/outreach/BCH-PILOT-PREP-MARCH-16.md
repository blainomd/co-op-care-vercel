3. **Project HEALS proves you already believe in this.** BCH Foundation funds 10 days / 4 hrs per day of companion support post-discharge. co-op.care is what happens on day 11 -- continuous, documented, physician-overseen companion care with no endpoint.

4. **Omaha-coded documentation is the differentiator.** Traditional agencies document "companionship -- 2 hours." co-op.care documents Omaha System problems with KBS scores, ICD-10 codes, and FHIR R4-structured data flowing back to BCH in real time. You get clinical visibility into post-discharge outcomes for the first time.

5. **Worker-owned caregivers stay.** Industry turnover is 79%. Cooperative home care companies run 22-38% turnover (JAMA Network Open, April 2025). Same caregiver, same patient, every visit. That continuity is what prevents the readmission.

## 4. Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "We already have BCH HomeCare and Project HEALS."
**Response:** BCH HomeCare is Class A skilled nursing -- complementary, not competitive. Project HEALS covers 10 days post-discharge. co-op.care is what happens after day 10: ongoing companion care with clinical documentation. We fill the gap between Project HEALS ending and the next ER visit. The question is: what happens to those patients today after HEALS support ends?

### Objection 2: "We'd need legal/compliance/IT review before any pilot."
**Response:** Understood. Phase 1 requires none of that. No data integration -- we use our own platform. No patient data flows from BCH to us. The discharge coordinator simply tells the patient about co-op.care as an option, same as they'd mention Visiting Angels or Home Instead. The patient opts in independently. At 90 days, we share de-identified aggregate outcomes. If those outcomes earn a deeper conversation, we bring in your compliance and IT teams then.

### Objection 3: "You're pre-revenue and pre-license. How do I know you'll exist in 90 days?"
**Response:** Fair question. Three things: (1) Josh Emdur, DO -- your own BCH hospitalist since 2008, 50-state licensed -- is co-founder and CMO. He's not going anywhere. (2) The technology is live today -- I'll show you on my phone in 60 seconds. (3) The pilot is zero cost to BCH. If we disappear, you've lost nothing. If we deliver, you've gained a post-acute partner with clinical-grade documentation that no agency in Boulder can match.

## 5. Demo Script (phone, careos-claude-code.vercel.app)

**Setup:** Have the site loaded on your phone before the meeting. Clear any previous session.

**Step 1: Open the site (10 sec)**
"Let me show you what a discharged patient's daughter would see."
Open careos-claude-code.vercel.app. Show the landing page. Point out: no medical jargon. Designed for a 49-year-old daughter at 11pm who just spent 3 hours caring for her mother.

**Step 2: Show the CareCard (15 sec)**
"When she signs up, she gets this -- her CareCard."
Show the card with its tappable tiles. Point out: each tile represents a domain of her mother's care -- mobility, nutrition, social contact, pain, cognition. The card visually improves as she engages. Empty state shows prompts, not blank fields.

**Step 3: Tap a tile (30 sec)**
"Watch what happens when she taps 'Mobility'."
Tap a tile. Sage responds conversationally -- asks about her mother's walking, fall risk, assistive devices. Point out: this is not a form. It is a conversation that auto-generates Omaha-coded clinical documentation in the background.

**Step 4: Show profile building (20 sec)**
"Every answer builds her mother's Living Profile in real time."
As Sage responds, point out how the profile fills in -- care recipient name, conditions, functional status. Say: "This is the data that traditional agencies never capture. Every conversation becomes a clinically structured encounter."

**Step 5: The clinical punchline (15 sec)**
"Here's what matters to you, Grant: every one of these conversations produces an Omaha System-coded encounter -- the same documentation standard used by the VNA of Omaha for 50 years. That data flows back to you as structured FHIR. For the first time, you'd have clinical visibility into what's happening with your patients after they leave this building."

**Total demo time: ~90 seconds.** Do not over-demo. Stop here. Let him ask questions.
