Organizational Overview

1.1 Applicant Description

co-op.care Technologies LLC (the "Hard Shell") is a Boulder, Colorado-based health technology company that provides enterprise-grade clinical AI infrastructure — the CareOS platform — to a network of worker-owned home care cooperatives (the "Soft Centers"). The Boulder Family Care Cooperative is the first local cooperative in this federated network, and will serve as the ACCESS Model participant entity for the Boulder County pilot.

co-op.care is uniquely positioned for the ACCESS Model because its cooperative ownership structure eliminates the 40–60% agency fee extraction that characterizes traditional home care — enabling substantially higher worker retention (projected 22% annual turnover vs. 66–79% industry average) and sustainable care continuity for chronic disease management.

1.2 Legal Structure & Medicare Enrollment

The Boulder Family Care Cooperative is being incorporated as a worker-owned cooperative under Colorado state law, with support from the Rocky Mountain Employee Ownership Center (RMEOC) and its Social Cooperative Academy. The cooperative will enroll in Medicare Part B as a Home Health Agency in advance of the July 2026 performance period start.

2.3 Health Equity Considerations

Boulder County's Medicare FFS population includes a meaningful proportion of lower-income seniors who face SDOH barriers (transportation, food security, social isolation) that compound chronic disease. The cooperative employment model directly addresses SDOH by employing caregivers from the same communities they serve — creating economic reciprocity and cultural trust. The Time Bank mutual aid layer (hOurworld platform) addresses non-clinical SDOH needs without additional billing cost.

Clinical Approach & Care Delivery Model

3.1 Core Clinical Model

co-op.care delivers a 5-stage integrated care workflow that combines AI-assisted documentation with mandatory physician oversight, structured around the Omaha System taxonomy for community-based care:

  1. Intake (SageCare.ai): 24/7 AI intake layer captures referrals from BCH discharge coordinators, auto-drafts individualized care plans within hours, and routes beneficiaries to the appropriate ACCESS track. Zero lead leakage — every referral is documented and acted upon.
  2. Assignment & Onboarding: Worker-owner caregivers are matched to beneficiaries based on clinical need, language, geography, and continuity potential. Caregiver onboarding uses CareAcademy (75-hour HHA certification, 59 online + 16 in-person) and Relias micro-learning modules for ongoing skill maintenance.
  3. Ongoing Care Delivery: Caregivers deliver scheduled home visits per the individualized care plan. The cooperative ownership model ensures care continuity — the 79% industry turnover rate drops to a projected 22% for worker-owners, preserving therapeutic relationships critical for chronic disease management.
  4. AI Clinical Documentation (4-Layer Safety Model):
    • Layer 1: Caregiver records ambient voice note at end of each visit
    • Layer 2: CareOS AI maps note to Omaha System KBS (Knowledge/Behavior/Status, 1–5 scale) and interRAI CAPs
    • Layer 3: Josh Emdur, DO (CMO) reviews and validates AI-generated clinical documentation via HIPAA-compliant Physician Oversight Portal — satisfying 2026 CMS AI oversight requirements
    • Layer 4: Validated data generates structured FHIR R4 output, populating Aidbox backend for billing, compliance, and outcome reporting
  5. Mutual Aid Support (Time Bank): Non-clinical needs (transportation, errands, companionship, SDOH) are addressed through the hOurworld Time Bank (TimeDollars: 1 hour of service = 1 credit), offloading these tasks from billable caregivers at zero additional cost.

3.3 Substitute Services Management

co-op.care will actively monitor Substitute Spend to maintain it below 90% of allowed substitute services — the threshold required to avoid payment reduction. The CareOS platform tracks digital health device billing codes (G0552, G0553), psychiatric diagnostic evaluations (90791, 90792), remote therapeutic monitoring setup (98975), and collaborative care management (99492) in real time, flagging any approaching thresholds for Clinical Director review.

4.2 Data Security & Privacy

All Protected Health Information (PHI) is processed and stored in HIPAA-compliant infrastructure. The Aidbox platform maintains SOC 2 Type II certification. The Physician Oversight Portal requires two-factor authentication and role-based access control. Caregiver mobile applications use end-to-end encryption for all voice note transmission.

4.3 AI Safety & Oversight (2026 CMS Requirements)

co-op.care's 4-layer clinical AI safety model is designed specifically to comply with CMS 2026 AI oversight requirements for AI-assisted medical necessity determinations:

  • No AI output is used for billing or clinical decision-making without physician validation (Layer 3)
  • Josh Emdur, DO (CMO, 50-state licensed) reviews every AI-generated clinical note via the HIPAA-compliant Physician Oversight Portal
  • Audit trail maintained for every AI inference, human review, and output modification
  • Omaha System KBS taxonomy provides standardized, validated clinical language — not free-form AI generation

Community Benefit & Health Equity

6.1 Economic Justice for Caregivers

The structural inequity in home care is not just a business problem — it is a public health problem. Caregiver poverty wages ($13–15/hr) drive the 79% annual turnover that makes chronic disease management impossible. co-op.care's cooperative model pays caregivers $22–27/hr, builds ~$52,000 in equity over 5 years for a 30-hour/week worker-owner, and eliminates the agency extraction that currently consumes 40–60% of every dollar families pay for care.

6.2 Precedent: Cooperative Home Care Associates (CHCA)

Cooperative Home Care Associates (Bronx, NY) — the largest worker-owned home care cooperative in the United States — has demonstrated for 40+ years that the cooperative model produces lower caregiver turnover, higher patient care quality, and measurable wealth building for workers. Peer-reviewed evidence published in Health Affairs Scholar (2025) confirms that cooperative structure improves both job quality and patient outcomes. co-op.care scales the CHCA model with enterprise AI infrastructure and federal ACCESS Model participation.

6.3 SDOH Integration via Time Bank

The hOurworld Time Bank (TimeDollars) layer addresses Social Determinants of Health that compound chronic disease without adding billing cost. Volunteers earn credits by providing transportation, meal support, and companionship — freeing worker-owner caregivers to focus exclusively on billable clinical care. This layer is unique to the cooperative model and has no analog in traditional home care agency operations.
