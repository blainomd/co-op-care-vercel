## 1. Mission & Thesis

**Mission:** Human capital that fights private equity.

**Thesis:** co-op.care is a worker-owned home care cooperative that uses physician-directed clinical governance and AI-assisted technology to deliver care that is structurally better for families, better for caregivers, and less expensive than anything else in the market. We are not a company entering a market. We are a community building its own care infrastructure.

**The Deflationary Insurgent:** HHS Deputy Secretary Chris Klomp has publicly stated that regulatory sandboxes are essential for allowing "insurgent" innovators to enter the healthcare market. The current CMS leadership views technology as a deflationary force required to "bend the cost curve." co-op.care is built to be that insurgent: every $35/hour cooperative visit that prevents a $2,500 ER visit or frees a $2,500/day blocked bed satisfies the core HHS objective.

**The Enemy:** Private equity firms have acquired hundreds of home care agencies. They cut caregiver pay to $13–17/hr, inflate billing to $30–45/hr, pocket the 40–60% spread, destroy continuity with 77% annual turnover, and flip the company in 3–5 years for IRR. They don't care about your mother. They care about their internal rate of return. co-op.care is structurally immune: worker ownership means there is no equity to extract, no company to flip, no wages to cut.

## 6. Legal Transformation — HSA/FSA

### The Mechanism
Under IRS Publication 502, custodial home care is NOT a qualified medical expense. But when a physician creates a care plan and signs a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN), the same care becomes "physician-directed" and qualifies for HSA/FSA pre-tax payment under Section 213(d) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### The Math

| | Without Josh's Signature | With Josh's Signature |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly care cost (20 hrs/wk × $35) | $3,031 | $3,031 |
| Payment method | After-tax dollars | Pre-tax HSA/FSA |
| Effective cost (28.5% bracket) | $3,031/month | **$2,167/month** |
| Annual cost | $36,372 | **$26,006** |
| **Family savings** | $0 | **$10,366/year** |

### Why No Traditional Agency Can Offer This
They don't have physician oversight. They don't have a Medical Director who reviews care plans and signs LMNs. They don't have the clinical governance infrastructure. This is co-op.care's structural moat — it requires a physician co-founder who believes in the mission.

### The LMN Template
Includes: patient info, ICD-10 diagnosis codes, CRI-14 domain scores (Physical, Cognitive 1.3× weight, Safety 1.1× weight, Social), medically necessary services checklist, frequency/duration, and IRS Pub 502 certification language. Josh signs. Family submits to HSA/FSA administrator.

## 8. Clinical Autonomy & Safety

### Clinical Autonomy Levels (CAL Framework)
Source: Doctronic presentation, March 5, 2026. Analogous to SAE J3016 vehicle automation.

| Level | Name | Description | co-op.care Application |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------------|
| L0 | No AI | Human doctors do all work | Traditional agency. Paper charts. |
| L1 | Observer | AI summarizes, reviews, extracts. No recommendations. | CareOS auto-documents visits, extracts Omaha codes |
| L2 | Advisor | AI recommends; clinician decides | CII/CRI generates recommendations. Blaine/Josh decides. |
| **L3** | **Co-signer** | **AI drafts; human review + signature required** | **co-op.care TODAY. AI drafts care plan. Blaine reviews. Josh signs.** |
| L4 | Conditional Autonomy | AI executes pre-approved protocols; escalates exceptions | Doctronic operates here. co-op.care target Phase 2. |
| L5 | Full Autonomy | AI handles all within defined scope | Near future for both companies |

### 4-Layer Safety Model

| Layer | Function | co-op.care Implementation |
|-------|----------|--------------------------|
| 1. Clinical Screening | Screen before any care plan generation | CRI-14: 14 weighted dimensions. AI cannot proceed without verified clinical context. |
| 2. Identity & Verification | Verify every encounter | EVV on every visit. GPS-confirmed. Member/caregiver credentials verified. |
| 3. Care History Cross-Check | Validate against longitudinal records | FHIR R4 pulls history. PointClickCare ADT cross-checks hospital events. |
| 4. Physician Oversight | Human authority on all clinical decisions | Josh reviews and signs. Any uncertainty → escalation. Family/caregiver override available. |

### Graduated Autonomy Protocol (Utah Model)
Source: Doctronic/Utah OAIP. "Autonomy expands only as safety data accumulates — modeled on physician residency training."

| Phase | Mode | Protocol |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Phase 1** | SUPERVISED | First 250 care plans: Josh reviews 100%. AI generates, physician approves every output. Pre-approval required. |
| **Phase 2** | MONITORED | Routine cases auto-transmit after safety checks. Josh retrospectively reviews 10%. |
| **Phase 3** | AUDITED | Statistical sampling (≥1% deviation at 95% confidence). Quarterly physician concordance analysis. Trend reporting to IAALS. |

### Escalation Triggers (Require Josh's Direct Involvement)
- CRI score > 75 (high acuity)
- Cognitive domain > 60 (dementia/behavioral)
- Fall event, hospitalization, or ER visit
- Medication interaction flag from CareOS
- Rapid clinical decline reported
- Member requests physician consultation

### Judgement Sufficiency Threshold
Source: Doctronic. "How do we decide to decide?" The physician defines the "acceptable decision space" (green circle). The AI operates strictly within it. Everything outside routes to human review. CRI-14 provides the minimum sufficient context for Josh to attest — he doesn't need to examine the patient. He needs sufficient structured clinical data.

## 14. Human Capital vs. Private Equity

### How PE Destroys Home Care
1. **Acquire:** Buy agency at 4–6× EBITDA
2. **Extract:** Cut caregiver wages from $18 to $13–14/hr. Eliminate benefits. Reduce training.
3. **Inflate:** Raise family billing from $30 to $40–45/hr. Add "assessment fees" and "administrative charges."
4. **Churn:** 77% annual turnover becomes a feature, not a bug — new caregivers don't get raises.
5. **Flip:** Sell in 3–5 years at higher EBITDA multiple. Community left with degraded care infrastructure.

### How co-op.care Inverts Every Step
1. **Build:** Community forms cooperative. Workers own 100%.
2. **Return:** $25–28/hr + benefits + equity. $52K over 5 years stays in community.
3. **Transparency:** $35/hr. Published. No hidden fees. Co-op books open.
4. **Retain:** <15% target turnover. Same caregiver every visit. Continuity = outcomes = revenue.
5. **Permanent:** Can't be bought. Can't be flipped. Can't be extracted. Worker ownership is structural immunity to PE.

### The Structural Advantages PE Cannot Replicate
- **HSA/FSA eligibility** — requires physician co-founder governance (not a vendor contract)
- **Community trust** — BCH, BVSD, credit union, Time Bank — requires years of embedding
- **Caregiver loyalty** — ownership ≠ employment. People don't leave what they own.
- **Regulatory sandbox** — requires IAALS relationship, physician oversight, FHIR R4, cooperative structure
- **Insurance data moat** — CII/CRI outcome data from cooperative members feeds underwriting. PE agencies don't have continuous clinical data.
- **2.6× local economic multiplier** — cooperative dollars recirculate locally. PE dollars leave.
