# FCC Pricing Analysis — The Honest Numbers

## Where "$3,500/mo" Comes From

The $3,500/month figure assumes approximately 27 hours/week of care at $30/hour:
- 27 hrs/week × $30/hr × 4.33 weeks/month = **$3,510/month**

At different care levels:
| Hours/Week | Monthly Cost at $30/hr |
|-----------|----------------------|
| 10 hrs | $1,300 |
| 15 hrs | $1,950 |
| 20 hrs | $2,600 |
| 25 hrs | $3,250 |
| 30 hrs | $3,900 |
| 40 hrs (full-time) | $5,200 |

**Important:** $3,500/mo is a midpoint estimate for part-time care, not a universal number.

## The Real FCC Value Propositions (Honest Framing)

### What IS genuinely different:

**1. Caregiver Retention (THE STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGE)**
- Industry: 77% annual caregiver turnover
- FCC projected: 15% (because caregivers are cooperative owners)
- What this means for families: You get the SAME caregiver month after month. Not a rotating door of strangers in your parent's home.
- What this means for health systems: Reliable discharge partners. When you refer a patient, the caregiver who starts is the one who stays.

**2. HSA Eligibility (REAL SAVINGS)**
- If FCC care qualifies as HSA-eligible medical expense, families effectively save 25-37% through tax advantage
- $3,500/mo pre-tax via HSA ≈ $2,275-2,625/mo after-tax equivalent
- THIS is where the genuine savings vs. traditional agencies show up
- Critical: Must verify HSA eligibility requirements with tax counsel

**3. Caregiver Quality (BETTER PAY = BETTER CARE)**
- Traditional agencies: $15-17/hr → attracts entry-level, high turnover
- FCC: $25-30/hr + equity + benefits → attracts experienced, committed caregivers
- The nursing literature consistently shows caregiver retention correlates with care quality

**4. Cooperative Model (ALIGNED INCENTIVES)**
- Traditional agency: profits from the spread between what families pay and what caregivers earn. Incentive = maximize spread.
- FCC: caregivers ARE the owners. No extraction. Incentive = deliver great care so families stay and cooperative grows.

### What is NOT genuinely different:

- **Per-hour pricing:** FCC at $30/hr is roughly market rate for Boulder, not dramatically cheaper
- **Home care vs nursing home cost:** Any home care is cheaper than a nursing home. This isn't unique to FCC.
- **Technology:** The "AI matching" and "smart scheduling" are nice-to-haves but not differentiators at launch

## How to Talk About Pricing in Conversations

### With Health Systems:
Lead with **reliability**, not price. "Your discharge planning team needs to know that when they refer a patient, the caregiver will actually show up — tomorrow and next month. Our cooperative model structurally solves the turnover problem."

### With Employers:
Lead with **HSA eligibility** and **hidden cost reduction**. "Your employees are already spending on caregiving — we make it tax-advantaged and professional, which reduces absenteeism."

### With Families:
Lead with **continuity of care** and **caregiver quality**. "Your mom deserves someone she knows and trusts, not a different stranger every week. Our caregivers earn $25-30/hr and own the company — they're not leaving."

### With Investors:
Lead with **the margin story**. "Traditional agencies extract 50-60% to fund corporate overhead. We operate as a cooperative with minimal overhead. That spread gets reinvested in caregiver wages, which drives retention, which drives quality, which drives growth. It's a virtuous cycle that's structurally impossible for traditional agencies to replicate."
