## How It Works

The Medical Director evaluates the member's conditions and writes a Letter of Medical Necessity specifying which community activities are medically necessary. Under IRS Publication 502, expenses for "the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease" qualify as medical expenses when prescribed by a physician.

co-op.care becomes the gateway that unlocks tax-advantaged wellness for the entire family.

**What qualifies under LMN:**
- Exercise programs prescribed for a specific diagnosed condition
- Nutrition counseling for diagnosed metabolic conditions
- Mental health programs for diagnosed anxiety, depression, or cognitive decline
- Therapeutic activities prescribed for fall prevention, balance, or mobility
- Social engagement programs prescribed for diagnosed social isolation contributing to health decline

**What does NOT qualify (even with LMN):**
- General wellness without a diagnosed condition
- Recreational activities not tied to a medical prescription
- Gym memberships without specific therapeutic prescription (gray area — IRS has historically denied general fitness)

### Retention
Once a family has an LMN covering co-op.care services AND community programs:

- Senior fitness is HSA-eligible
- Nutrition counseling is HSA-eligible
- Professional care is HSA-eligible

That's retention through value creation, not lock-in.
