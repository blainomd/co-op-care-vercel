## Source 5: Community Wellness — The LMN Gateway

### How It Works

The Medical Director evaluates the member's condition and writes a Letter of Medical Necessity that specifies which community activities are medically necessary. Under IRS Publication 502, medical expenses include "the cost of participating in a weight-loss program for a specific disease diagnosed by a physician," therapeutic exercise prescribed for a medical condition, and other qualifying treatments.

When the LMN covers yoga for fall prevention, tai chi for balance and cognitive function, senior fitness for cardiovascular health, nutrition counseling for diabetes management, or meditation for anxiety and cognitive decline — those activities become qualified medical expenses payable from the family's Health Savings Account or Flexible Spending Account.

co-op.care becomes the **gateway** that unlocks tax-advantaged wellness for the entire family.

## Revenue Implications

### New revenue stream: Care Navigation Fee
The assessment, LMN, and community referral coordination is itself a billable service. When the Medical Director evaluates a member, writes a comprehensive LMN covering co-op.care services AND community wellness referrals, and the care team coordinates the full plan — that's a $300-500 comprehensive care assessment and navigation package.

### LMN as a retention mechanism
Once the family has an LMN that makes yoga, tai chi, nutrition, and professional care all HSA-eligible, they're deeply embedded in the co-op.care ecosystem. Switching to a traditional agency means losing the LMN — and the tax advantage on everything.

### Community partners as referral pipeline
Every yoga studio, senior fitness program, nutrition counselor, and community center in Boulder becomes a referral partner. "Your students who need home care — we can help. And we'll keep sending our members to your programs." The relationship is bidirectional.

### Training as revenue
Caregiver training (transfers, medication management, dementia techniques) is a service the Conductor pays for — and it's potentially HSA-eligible under the LMN. A weekend caregiver training workshop at $200-500 per family is both revenue and customer deepening.
