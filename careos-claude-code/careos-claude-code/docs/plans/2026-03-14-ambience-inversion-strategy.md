# The Ambience Inversion — co-op.care Strategic Positioning
## Date: March 14, 2026

---

## Context

Ambience Healthcare has proven that AI-generated clinical documentation changes hospital operating margins ($30M net new margin at one center). Their model: sell AI scribe tools to health systems, prove ROI to CFOs, and expand to full platform.

co-op.care replicates the same AI capabilities but inverts the business model: instead of selling tools to care delivery organizations, we ARE the care delivery organization. This is structurally stronger.

---

## The Structural Comparison

| Dimension | Health AI Vendors (Ambience, Abridge) | co-op.care |
|---|---|---|
| Business model | SaaS vendor to health systems | Technology-enabled care delivery company |
| Revenue source | Software contracts | LMNs + care services + (later) platform licensing |
| Proof of value | Customer case studies, 6-month conversion | Own P&L — undeniable |
| Sales cycle | 6-12 months per health system | Zero — deploy to ourselves |

| Moats | Technology (1 moat) | Technology + Operations + Ownership (3 moats) |

## The Amazon/AWS Analogy

Amazon didn't build AWS to sell cloud computing. They built it to run Amazon.com. Then they realized the infrastructure they'd built for themselves was more valuable than anyone else's because it was battle-tested on the world's largest e-commerce operation.

co-op.care doesn't build CareOS to sell to home care agencies. We build it to run co-op.care. Then in Year 2-3, when we have 12 months of operational data proving it works, we license it to every other home care company — and our pitch is: "This is what it does to OUR margins. Want this?"

## The Three Moats

### Moat 1: Technology
CareOS platform — Sage AI, AmbientScribe, ALGS, Omaha-to-FHIR mapping, Living Profile, Video Home Assessment. This is the same moat any AI company has. It's necessary but insufficient alone — technology can be copied.

### Moat 2: Operations
We employ the caregivers. We serve the families. We hold the cooperative charter. We have the physician co-founder with 50-state licensure. No amount of code copying gives a competitor these relationships. This moat deepens with every month of operation.

### Moat 3: Ownership Structure
Worker-owners have equity. When co-op.care deploys a new tool (AmbientScribe, Video Home Assessment), caregivers are incentivized to adopt it because better tools → better outcomes → better cooperative revenue → their equity appreciates. This is built-in change management. Ambience has to convince 500 doctors at a hospital to change behavior. We have 5 caregivers who own the company.

## The Licensing Flywheel

### Year 1: Operate
- Run co-op.care on CareOS in Colorado
- Generate LMN revenue nationally (Josh's 50-state license)
- Track every operational metric obsessively:
  - Cost per structured clinical observation (before/after AmbientScribe)
  - LMN draft accuracy (% approved without modification)
  - Caregiver documentation time saved per shift
  - KBS outcome improvement per client
  - Revenue per Sage conversation (conversation → CII → LMN → HSA savings)
  - Caregiver turnover rate (the cooperative thesis test)

### Year 2: Prove
- 12 months of operational data on our own P&L
- Publish results: "Here's what CareOS does to home care margins"
- Begin conversations with other home care agencies and emerging cooperatives
- Ambience charges $3-5K/provider/year. CareOS pricing TBD but based on per-caregiver/month model

### Year 3: License (Federation)
- Other cooperatives deploy CareOS
- Platform fee: SaaS per caregiver/month
- Data network effects: more Omaha observations → better AI → better LMN drafts for all
- Federation governance: co-op.care is the "franchisor" of worker-owned home care
- Revenue: care delivery + LMN + platform licensing (three streams)

## Ambience as Phase 2-3 Partner

Ambience owns the hospital visit. co-op.care owns the home visit. These are complementary, not competitive:

- **Phase 1 (now):** AmbientScribe uses Whisper + Claude at near-zero cost
- **Phase 2-3:** Plug into Ambience API for:
  - Clinical-grade speaker diarization (who said what)
  - AutoCDI for HCC risk flags (PACE revenue optimization)
  - $3-5K/provider/year — justified when PACE sub-capitation revenue covers it
- **Long-term:** Ambience handles hospital-side clinical data, CareOS handles community/home-side. Shared FHIR R4 interoperability layer. Together = full-spectrum clinical intelligence from hospital bed to home.
