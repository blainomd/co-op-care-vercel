## CHANGE 1: The Conductor Reframe

**Old framing:** co-op.care serves aging adults. The Alpha Daughter is a marketing demographic.
**New framing:** The Alpha Daughter IS the customer. She is the Conductor — using AI-powered tools to understand care needs, make better decisions, and orchestrate the best possible care for her loved one. The platform is built around HER, not the aging adult as the end user.

### What this changes:

**All infographics — hero text:**
- Old: "Worker-owned home care cooperative"
- New: "Built for the family caregiver. The daughter 800 miles away who needs to understand her parent's care, make better decisions, and orchestrate the right support — without quitting her job or losing her mind."

**Conductor Dashboard descriptions (all versions):**
- Old: "For the Alpha Daughter — the remote caregiver orchestrating support"
- New: "Your command center. See how Mom is doing. Understand what she needs. Make better decisions about her care. Coordinate professional caregivers and neighbors. Get a daily briefing that tells you she's okay — or that she isn't, and what's being done about it."

**CII Assessment positioning:**
- Old: "Measures family caregiver burnout"
- New: "The first tool that takes YOUR experience seriously. Before we assess your parent's needs, we assess yours — because you can't conduct the orchestra if you're collapsing."

**CRI Assessment positioning:**
- Old: "Quantifies the aging adult's care needs"
- New: "Gives YOU the clarity to make informed decisions. The numbers and the language to have the conversation with your siblings, your financial planner, and your physician."

**Revenue/investor language:**
- Old: "Families save 44%"
- New: "The Conductor saves 44% — and gets the information, the tools, and the team she needs to stop improvising and start orchestrating."

**Outreach — Alpha Daughter emails:**
- Old: "Your mom's care..."
- New: "You've been carrying this alone. co-op.care is built for you — the person managing the care, making the decisions, and holding everything together."

**Content posts — all family-facing:**
- Lead with HER, not Mom. Mom's needs are the context. The daughter's experience is the story.

## CHANGE 2: Subcontractor Revenue Model

**Old framing:** co-op.care bills Medicare via CHI/PIN codes, participates in ACCESS/LEAD directly.
**New framing:** co-op.care does not bill Medicare or Medicaid. It becomes the indispensable delivery partner for entities that do. They capture the federal savings. We capture a B2B contract.

### What this changes:

**All revenue charts:**

| Old Label | New Label |
|-----------|-----------|
| Medicare CHI/PIN | Physician practice subcontract (care coordination) |
| Hospital Retainers | Hospital discharge partnership |
| ACCESS OAPs | Federal innovation program (via ACO partner) |
| LEAD Shared Savings | LEAD/CARA Preferred Provider (via ACO subcontract) |
| MAHA ELEVATE | Federal cooperative grant (if awarded) |

**Add new layers not in original charts:**

| New Layer | Description | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| MA Supplemental Benefits | Per-hour or PMPM vendor contract with Medicare Advantage plan | 2026 |
| Post-Discharge TCM Partnership | Practice bills $235/episode; Co-op.care delivers in-home follow-up | 2026 |
| GUIDE Respite Subcontract | GUIDE participant bills CMS; Co-op.care delivers dementia respite | 2027 |
| CHW Medicaid Billing | Direct Medicaid billing for certified Community Health Workers | 2028+ |

**Revenue confidence legend:**
- Green: Direct commercial (private pay, memberships, assessments)
- Teal: Institutional subcontracts with existing programs (MA supplemental, PACE, TCM)
- Gold: Institutional subcontracts requiring new federal programs (LEAD/CARA, GUIDE)
- Red: Upside requiring licensing or grant awards (insurance, MAHA ELEVATE, Medicaid)

**Investor deck and infographic headline numbers:**
- Old: "$2.6M core / $4.1M full scenario"
- New: "$3.8M core + institutional / $4.8M full scenario"
- The difference: PACE and MA supplemental moved from "upside" to "institutional subcontract" tier

**All "10-layer revenue stack" references:**
- Old: "10 layers"
- New: Don't count layers. Say: "Multiple revenue streams from one visit — direct commercial, institutional subcontracts, and federal program partnerships. We capture data once and route it across every payment rail simultaneously."

**The governing thesis line (use everywhere):**
"They capture the federal savings. We capture a B2B contract."

## CHANGE 3: Hard Scope, Warm Handoff, Relationship Continuity

**Old framing:** Scope limitations mentioned but not positioned as a feature.
**New framing:** "Clean scope is what makes us a safe partner for everyone — hospitals, home health agencies, ACOs, and families. We're additive to everyone's model, not competitive with anyone's."

### What this changes:

**All partner-facing materials:**
- Add: "We are custodial care, and that's a feature. 80% of aging in place is personal care — the market that's broken. When the other 20% shows up, we have curated referral partnerships and we stay in the picture."

**All family-facing materials:**
- Add: "The family doesn't start over with strangers at the worst possible moment."
- The escalation section in every infographic should use the exact phrase: "Hard scope, warm handoff, relationship continuity."

**Scope of Practice table (all versions):**
- Add a footer: "This boundary protects you. It's what allows us to be a safe partner for your hospital, your doctor, and your family — and it's why those institutions will refer to us."

**Physician-facing materials:**
- Add: "We don't compete with home health agencies. We complement them. When our member needs skilled care, we refer to your preferred providers and stay as the continuity anchor. That makes us a referral partner, not a competitor."

**For the Optum/MA/ACO/LEAD outreach:**
- Add: "Our custodial scope is a feature for your risk model. We handle the 80% of aging-at-home that's personal care. When skilled needs arise, we refer to your network's home health providers and maintain continuity. Clean scope means clean contracting — no overlap, no scope creep, no compliance ambiguity."

## LINE-LEVEL FIXES

### Signature block
- Remove "MPH" unless verified: `Blaine Warkentine, MD` (not MD MPH)
- Standardize across all drafts: `Blaine Warkentine, MD | blaine@co-op.care | 484-684-5287 | co-op.care`

### Caregiver number
- Use: "53 million" (NAC/AARP 2020)
- Do NOT use: "93 million" (broader definition, will be challenged)

### SB 24-205 enforcement date
- VERIFY before any material goes external. Footnote 40 in Blueprint 1 (Akin Gump) suggests possible postponement. If postponed, compliance timeline changes.

### Age at Home Insurance language
- Always include: "Age at Home is our cooperative long-term care insurance product, planned for launch in 2027. It is not a licensed insurance product today."
- Never position the $100 founding family deposit as an insurance premium

### CRI scoring constants
- Corrected: Min raw = 14.4, Max raw = 72.0 (not 13.4/67.0 as in original Blueprint)
- Tests pass with corrected values

### "co-op.care" capitalization
- Always lowercase: co-op.care (not Co-op.Care, not Co-Op.Care)
- Exception: beginning of a sentence where style guides require capitalization
